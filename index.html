Everyday ableist language

Ableism is more than language. It is systemic. Removing ableist language from our vocabularies is part of the process of tackling ableism. Below are common, everyday ableist phrases along with alternatives to them. The ableist phrases are arranged alphabetically.

(Note: some of the words below are slurs; others - like blind and ADHD - are accurate descriptors for people who are blind, etc., but become ableist when used outside of that context, to describe negative events, attitudes, etc.)

For more information on ableism, read this webpage from the charity Sense: Ableism and disablism. And this webpage from Access Living: Ableism 101.
